Use `Connections` in `Settings` to manage the external accounts Adtool can access, including Meta/Facebook and Google Drive. Use this page when you need to connect, reconnect, disconnect, or check why an account is not available in Adtool.

## What connections mean in Adtool

Connections are the permissions that allow Adtool to access external tools on your behalf. They determine which connected resources Adtool can show and use during workflows such as selecting Meta assets or importing creatives from Google Drive.

| **Connection** | **What it can affect** |
| --- | --- |
| Meta/Facebook | Access to Meta-related accounts, pages, and ad workflow resources available to your user. |
| Google Drive | Access to Google Drive files used when importing creatives. |

<Note>
  The exact accounts and assets you can see depend on both the connection in Adtool and the permissions your user has in the connected platform.
</Note>

## What disconnecting or reconnecting affects

| **Action** | **What to expect** |
| --- | --- |
| Reconnect | Refreshes the connection when permissions are expired, missing, or need to be granted again. |
| Disconnect | Removes that connection from Adtool. Related resources may stop appearing until you connect again. |

Disconnecting a connection does not automatically change permissions inside Meta/Facebook or Google Drive. It only changes whether Adtool has an active connection for that service.

## What to do if an asset does not appear within Adtool

If an asset such as an ad account or Page is missing inside Adtool, check the access source and the Adtool connection:

| **Check** | **Why it matters** |
| --- | --- |
| Your Meta user has access to the missing ad account or Page inside Meta | Adtool can only show Meta assets that the connected Meta user can access. |
| The relevant Meta connection is connected in Adtool | Open `Settings` \> `Connections` and confirm the Meta connection is active. Reconnect Meta if the connection is expired or missing. |

## Common cases

<Expandable title="My Meta/Facebook ad account or page is missing">
  Open `Settings`, go to `Connections`, and reconnect Meta/Facebook. If it still does not appear, check that your Meta user has access to the missing asset in Meta and that the required permissions were approved during connection.
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="My Google Drive files are not available">
  Open `Settings`, go to `Connections`, and reconnect Google Drive. Then confirm the connected Google user has access to the files or folders you are trying to use.
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="I changed permissions in Meta or Google Drive">
  Reconnect the relevant connection in Adtool so the updated permissions can be reflected in your Adtool workflows.
</Expandable>
